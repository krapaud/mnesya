# Mnesya — User Guide

Mnesya is a reminder app designed to help **elderly people** manage their daily tasks, supported by a **caregiver** (family member, nurse, or carer) who sets up and monitors reminders remotely.

---

## Who is this guide for?

This guide covers **both user types**:

- **Caregiver** — sets up profiles, creates reminders, monitors status
- **User (elderly person)** — receives reminders and responds to them on their device

---

## Caregiver — Getting Started

### 1. Create an account

1. Open the app and tap **Sign up**
2. Enter your first name, last name, email, and a password
3. Tap **Create account**

> Password requirements: at least 8 characters, 1 uppercase letter, 1 number, 1 special character (e.g. `!`, `@`, `#`).

---

### 2. Log in

1. Tap **Log in**
2. Enter your email and password
3. Tap **Sign in**

Your session lasts **60 minutes**. The app refreshes your token automatically — you will rarely need to log in again.

---

### 3. Create a user profile (elderly person)

1. From the dashboard, tap **Create profile**
2. Enter the elderly person's first name, last name, and date of birth
3. Tap **Save**

A **pairing code** is automatically generated and shown on screen. Share this 6-character code with the elderly person — they will need to enter it on their device to link it to their profile.

> The pairing code is valid for **5 minutes**. You can generate a new one at any time from the profile detail screen.

---

### 4. Create a reminder

1. From the dashboard, select a user profile
2. Tap **Add reminder**
3. Enter a title (e.g. "Take medication"), an optional description, and pick a date and time
4. Tap **Save**

The system will send a push notification to the elderly person's device at the scheduled time.

If there is no response, **two automatic follow-ups** are sent (at +5 min and +10 min). After 10 minutes without a response, **you receive an alert**.

---

### 5. Monitor reminders

From the **Reminders** tab, you can:

- View all reminders for all your users
- Filter by date, user, or status
- See the current status: **Pending**, **Done**, **Postponed**, **Unable**

---

### 6. Edit your profile

Tap the **Profile** tab, then **Edit** to update your name, email, or password.

---

## User (Elderly Person) — Getting Started

### 1. Pair your device

1. Open the app and tap **I have a code**
2. Enter the **6-character code** your caregiver shared with you
3. Tap **Confirm**

Your device is now linked to your profile. You will not need to log in again — the app remembers you for **90 days**.

---

### 2. Home screen

The home screen shows:

- Your **name**
- Your **next upcoming reminder**
- A simple greeting

---

### 3. Responding to a reminder

When a reminder arrives, a large notification screen appears:

| Button        | Meaning                        |
| ------------- | ------------------------------ |
| ✅ **Done**   | I have completed the task      |
| ⏰ **Later**  | I will do it later (postponed) |
| ❌ **Unable** | I cannot do it                 |

Tap one of the three buttons to respond. Your caregiver will see your response in their app.

---

### 4. Push notifications

Make sure **notifications are enabled** for Mnesya in your device settings:

- **iOS:** Settings → Mnesya → Notifications → Allow
- **Android:** Settings → Apps → Mnesya → Notifications → Enable

---

## Frequently Asked Questions

**I forgot my password.**  
The current version does not include a password reset by email. Contact your system administrator to reset your account.

**My pairing code expired.**  
Ask your caregiver to generate a new one. It only takes a few seconds from the profile screen.

**I'm not receiving notifications.**  
Check that notifications are enabled in your device settings (see above). Make sure the app has been opened at least once after installation to register the device token.

**The app says my session has expired.**  
Tap **Log in** and sign back in with your email and password (caregiver only). Elderly users are not affected — their token lasts 90 days.

**I accidentally tapped the wrong button on a reminder.**  
Statuses cannot be undone from the user screen. Ask your caregiver to update the record from their dashboard if needed.
